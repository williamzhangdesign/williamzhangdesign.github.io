MI Safe Start Map

This is the official COVID-19 dashboard for Michigan, commissioned by the State of Michigan
and built by the UM School of Information and School of Public Health.

Timeline: Ongoing, April 2020 - Present

Roles: Co-designer, Co-researcher

Live dashboard screenshot as of October 21, 2020

Context

Problem

The State of Michigan needs a data-backed resource to decide whether to initiate statewide re-opening procedures or reinstate shut-down procedures amidst the COVID-19 pandemic.

Audience

The dashboard tracks COVID-19's public health indicators for Michigan. The two primary indicators displayed on the dashboard are Epidemic Spread and Public Health Capacity.

There are 2 versions of the dashboard serving 2 audiences:

  1. ⦿  Private Version: Government officials and public health experts making decisions
  2. ⦿  Public Version: The general public trying to make sense of decisions

Because the private version has many niche requirements accompanied by high-frequency dashboard-use and training for users, stakeholders prioritize complex feature requirements over general user experience.

On the other hand, UX has a much larger impact and brings significantly more value for the public version's broader user population. My work with the dashboard has primarily focuses on the public version.
